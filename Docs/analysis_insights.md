## Key Finding 1 — Grade Drives Everything
Default rates rise predictably with grade:
- Grade A: 6.61% concluded default rate
- Grade G: 52.20% concluded default rate
- Grade G may be unprofitable even at 28% interest rates

## Key Finding 2 — FICO Predicts Default
Default rate nearly quadruples from Exceptional (7.42%)
to Fair (28.14%) FICO bands.
670-739 Good band = 71% of entire portfolio.

## Key Finding 3 — Portfolio Deteriorated 2013-2018
Default rates climbed from 15.60% (2013) to 28.58% (2018)
as loan volume grew from 134K to 495K annually.
2016 was the worst single-year deterioration (+4.29pp).

## Key Finding 4 — Geography Is an Independent Risk Factor
Mississippi (29.25%) has nearly double DC's default rate (14.40%)
despite similar FICO scores and DTI ratios.
Economic environment matters beyond individual creditworthiness.

## Key Finding 5 — Small Business Loans Are Highest Risk
32.04% default rate — 10 points above portfolio average.
Personal loans used for business carry compounded risk.

## Key Finding 6 — Missing Data Is a Risk Signal
Borrowers with no employment length specified default at 29.28%
vs 20.28% for 10+ year employed borrowers.

## Key Finding 7 — Defaulters Look Almost Like Repayers
Average difference between defaulted and fully paid borrowers:
- FICO: 688 vs 698 (10 points)
- Income: $70K vs $77K (9% lower)
- Interest rate: 15.71% vs 12.63% (correctly priced higher)
- Revolving utilisation: 54.76% vs 51.08%

## Key Finding 8 — DTI Predicts Default
Default rate doubles from Low DTI (16.43%)
to Extreme DTI 40%+ (38.45%).
